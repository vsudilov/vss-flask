My research involving Magnesium -- Click each section to get more info

During a star's lifetime and death, it ejects gases. The gases from many stars accumulate in their host galaxy. From measurements of these such gases, we can construct a picture about the composition of even distant galaxies. These gases are a fossil record of a galaxy's history.

One important gas is MgII, which is once ionized magnesium. Magnesium is produced in abundance by nuclear reactions taking place in stars.
